The Story

Built by someone who fought the same tools you're fighting

The county CAMA system was fine. It held the data. It could show you a record. But it couldn't answer a question. It couldn't tell you what was actually happening in a neighborhood, why land values were behaving the way they were, or whether your assumptions were holding up against the data.

The work started small — custom filters to get related data on one screen. Then data exports to Excel for closer inspection. Then R and RStudio to test assumptions and see what the numbers were actually saying. And when our revaluation cycle came around, the first real tool: a vacant land explorer built to help sift through thousands of possible comps to identify the best available. A tool to help appraisers develop accurate, defensible neighborhood land values.

That's where this all started. Not in a computer lab, not in a conference room, but in the middle of a complex ongoing revaluation. Mass appraisal tools built for real data, real deadlines, and the real world.
